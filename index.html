Research

My current research interest is to build co-evolving intelligence to tackle health challenges. Health problems are inherently uncertain, rarely admitting a single fixed answer; they vary across individuals, shift over time, and depend on context that no static model can fully anticipate. Addressing this uncertainty demands AI systems that learn from real-world human interaction, refine their reasoning through feedback, and adapt to the individual they serve. Yet an AI that truly adapts to a person can no longer remain a generic tool. As it shapes the care a person receives, it is in turn shaped by every encounter, growing in a way that is inseparable from the life it serves. Such a system is not merely built for lives; it itself lives.

Biography

I joined Tsinghua University as a tenure-track assistant professor and principal investigator in biomedical AI. Previously, I served as the founding member and head of AI systems at a2z Radiology AI, and as a postdoctoral fellow at Harvard University with Professor Pranav Rajpurkar. I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Hong Kong, advised by Professor Yizhou Yu. I spent two years at Tencent as a research scientist, and received degrees from Nanjing University and Wuhan University.
