About me

I’m a 2nd-year graduate student under the supervision of Prof. Jianxin Wu, at the LAMDA group in Nanjing University.

I got my bachelor degree in Survey and Mapping from Wuhan University. During the first two years in Whu, I tried to figure out the effects of orbit errors of GPS (Global Positioning System) under the supervision of Prof. Yun Wu. Then I spent time as a research intern at LIESMARS working on point cloud processing in SLAM (Simultaneous Localization And Mapping), and I helped to implement several algorithms in a real robot to perform such tasks (like navigation, etc).

Research

I’m interested in algorithms for computer vision and deep learning. Nowadays, I have focused on the area of object detection, where I have been trying to improve the efficiency and robustness of modern detectors.

Besides, I also hold an interest in fine-grained classification problems and vision approaches in remote sensing.
